What Actually Makes a College Fight Song “Hype”?

College fight songs mapped by musical speed and lyrical aggression.

Fight songs are designed to energize crowds, but not all of them do it the same way. This chart maps songs by how fast they are and how aggressively their lyrics repeat traditional hype tropes. Together, these dimensions reveal four distinct styles of hype.

How hype styles extend beyond the song

Fight songs shape how hype is expressed, but they exist within broader home-field environments. To explore whether hype styles tend to appear in more intimidating settings, we combine recent home win rates (2021–2025) and stadium capacity into a standardized measure of stadium difficulty. The distribution below shows how Big Ten schools (n = 16 programs) in each hype style are spread across low-, medium-, and high-difficulty home environments.

Takeaway: Aggressive and hybrid hype styles appear more often in difficult home environments, while speed-driven styles skew toward easier ones.

How the analysis works
  • Fight song data: FiveThirtyEight (tempo and lyrical tropes)
  • Hype styles defined by dataset medians (BPM and trope count)
  • Stadium difficulty score: standardized home win rate (2021–2025) plus standardized stadium capacity; schools are grouped into low, medium, and high tiers using terciles of this score.
  • Secondary analysis focuses on Big Ten programs
