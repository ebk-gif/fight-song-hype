What Actually Makes a College Fight Song “Hype”?

Every college fight song is meant to hype a crowd, but not every crowd gets hyped the same way. This project examines how tempo and lyrical intensity shape four distinct styles of hype.

Takeaway: College fight song hype is not one-dimensional. Some songs prioritize speed over aggression, others emphasize repetition and intensity, and only a subset combine both, revealing distinct stylistic tradeoffs in how schools generate crowd energy.

Hype Beyond the Song

Fight songs shape how hype is expressed, but they also operate within broader social contexts. Here, the competitive and psychological dynamics of home-field environments. To examine how the four hype styles identified above appear across different competitive contexts, we combined recent home win rates (2021–2025) and stadium capacity into a standardized measure of stadium difficulty. The distribution below shows how Big Ten schools (n = 18 programs) within each hype style are spread across low-, medium-, and high-difficulty home environments.

Takeaway: Aggressive and hybrid hype styles appear more often in difficult home environments, while speed-driven styles skew toward easier ones.

How the analysis works
  • Fight song data: FiveThirtyEight (tempo and lyrical tropes)
    • FiveThirtyEight fight songs dataset (tempo and lyrical tropes)
  • Big Ten subset and derived measures: processed project dataset
  • Hype styles defined by dataset medians (BPM and trope count)
  • Stadium difficulty score: standardized home win rate (2021–2025) plus standardized stadium capacity; schools are grouped into low, medium, and high tiers using terciles of this score.
  • Secondary analysis focuses on Big Ten programs
